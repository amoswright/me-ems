Allergy/Anaphylaxis #4

IV EPINEPHrine infusion drip, administered via a Maine EMS-approved medication pump, should be started for patients unresponsive to IM EPINEPHrine administration in either of the following settings:

  • Cardiovascular collapse (hypotension with altered mental status, pallor, diaphoresis, or delayed capillary refill)
    OR
  • Hypotension that is unresponsive to a total of 60 mL/kg (3 x 20 mL/kg boluses) isotonic fluid boluses and repeat doses of IM EPINEPHrine

Evidence does not support the prophylactic use of EPINEPHrine in the asymptomatic patient after exposure to a known allergen.

Children experiencing cutaneous signs of an allergic reaction with no systemic symptoms should be evaluated by a medical clinician.

Children experiencing anaphylaxis in the prehospital setting who receive EPINEPHrine require EMS transport.
