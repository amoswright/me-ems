Chest Pain - Suspected Cardiac Origin #2 & STEMI Criteria

Apply defib pads and be prepared to defibrillate as these patients are high risk for VF/VT

  1. Patients who present with inferior MI, clear lung sounds, and BP less than 90 mmHg, give fluid bolus.

  2. Consider
    a. Additional nitroglycerin
    b. Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg IV/IM, or IN to a MAX dose of 100 mcgs
    c. Additional fluid bolus in patients with inferior MI

  3. If patient develops a dysrhythmia, refer to appropriate protocol.

The management of STEMI patients is commonly guided by 2 possible time goals:

  1. 90 minutes from first medical contact to device deployment in the coronary artery, and
  2. 30 minutes from the hospital arrival to treatment with thrombolytics

In most settings, these goal establish cascading treatment possibilities. For instance, if both a catheterization strategy and a thrombolytic strategy can be accomplished within the above goals, catheterization is preferred. If catheterization cannot be accomplished with the above goals, but a thrombolytic strategy an be accomplished, a thrombolytic strategy may be preferred. If neither goal can be be accomplished within the established goals, consider the strategy that is most likely to open the artery as soon as possible.*

*Destination guidance considers availability of local resources, geography, clinician licensure-level, patient stability, comorbidities and many other factors. EMS clinicians are encouraged to work closely with their service medical director as well as their regional medical director and leadership from the local STEMI system of care to determine the best treatment strategy.
