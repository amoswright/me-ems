Maine Death with Dignity Law

PEARLS
Death with Dignity Law (Sec. 1. 22 MRS c. 418 ): The Maine Death with Dignity Act provides eligible Maine residents with terminal, incurable diseases that will, within reasonable medical judgment, result in death within six months, the option to be prescribed a dose of medication that, if taken, will hasten the end of their life.

Patients should have a either a form that identifies their participation in the Maine Death with Dignity Program or a DNR/POLST form.

It is possible that Maine EMS personnel may be called to respond to an individual who has voluntarily entered into an agreement with his/her Attending physician to end their life under the Death with Dignity Act.

If dispatched to such a patient, and questions arise regarding patient care, please contact OLMC.
