Transport of Mentally Ill Patients #2

Pediatric behavioral health emergencies are increasing in frequency throughout the United States. EMS clinicians should be compassionate to the special needs and considerations of this population, especially in times of crisis. Establishing rapport with the patient, speaking at eye level, and using a non-threatening physical posture should be emphasized when de-escalating these calls. The clinician will often find it helpful to engage the parents/guardians in the evaluation and transport decision.
