Poisoning/Overdose #2

  1. Suggested Treatments:
    1. Symptomatic bradycardia (hypotension, altered mental status, syncope/presyncope, chest pain, dyspnea, acute heart failure, signs of shock, or cyanosis/pallor) due to beta- or calcium channel blocker overdose:
      1. Adult: Calcium gluconate 3 grams IV/IO, diluted in 50–100 mL of IVF, over 15 minutes, via IV pump; may repeat every 10 min for up to 4 additional doses, as needed.
      2. Pediatric: Calcium gluconate 60 mg/kg IV/IO, diluted in 50–100 mL of IVF, over 15 minutes, via IV pump (MAX 3 gm/dose); may repeat every 10 min for up to 4 additional doses, as needed.
      3. Refer to Adult Bradycardia, Red 29 or Pediatric Bradycardia Red 31, protocol
    2. Dystonic reaction:
      1. Adult: Diphenhydramine 25–50 mg IV/IM
      2. Pediatric: Diphenhydramine 1–2 mg/kg IV/IM (MAX dose 50 mg)
    3. Organophosphates: Nerve Agent/Organophosphate/Carbamate Poisoning protocol, Yellow 7
    4. Severe agitation: Hyperactive Delirium with Severe Agitation protocol, Orange 5
    5. Tricyclic Antidepressant (TCA)/sodium-channel blocker* overdose with either hemodynamic instability or widened QRS complex on initial 12-lead ECG defined as:
      1. Tachycardia (Adult: heart rate greater than 100 bpm; Pediatric as defined by age, see Pink 4) AND,
        1. QRS greater than 120 msec, OR
        2. An increase in QRS of 10 msec over serial 12-lead ECGs (repeat every 10 min, if feasible).
      2. Repeat 12-lead ECG after treatment and every 10 minutes (if feasible) if QRS is less than 120 msec, and every 5 minutes (if feasible) if QRS is greater than 120 msec. Treat as follows:
        1. Sodium Bicarbonate:
          1. Adult: 1 mEq/kg with a MAX of 100 mEq IV push over 1 minute. May repeat as needed with goal of QRS complex less than 120 msec. MAX of three doses.
          2. Pediatric: 1 mEq/kg with a MAX of 100 mEq IV push over 1 minute. May repeat as needed with goal of QRS complex less than 120 msec. MAX of three doses. (8.4% sodium bicarbonate must be diluted 1:1 with D5W to 4.2% [0.5 mEq/mL] prior to administration in patients less than 2 years of age)
        2. Fluid bolus for hypotension
        3. NOREPInephrine:
          • Preparation – mix NOREPInephrine 8 mg in 250 mL NS [32 mcg/mL]
          • Dosing – Usual starting dose of NOREPInephrine is 0.03 mcg/kg/min. Clinical circumstances may require a slightly higher starting dose of 0.06–0.09 mcg/kg/min. Titrate by 0.03 mcg/kg/min every 3 minutes to meet desired clinical effect, which may include appropriate SBP:
            1. Adult: systolic greater than 90 mmHg and/or MAP greater than 65.
