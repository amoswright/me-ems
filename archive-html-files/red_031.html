Pediatric patients are considered bradycardic if HR is less than 60 beats/min despite adequate oxygenation and ventilation. In children, bradycardia almost always reflects hypoxia rather than a primary cardiac problem. It is a pre-arrest rhythm and the prognosis is poor if left untreated. Immediate delivery of oxygen and assisted ventilations are essential. Untreated bradycardia will quickly cause shock, hypotension and death. Consider causes of bradycardia, including hypoxia, hypothermia, hypoglycemia, beta blocker or calcium channel blocker overdose, and hyperkalemia.

For a newborn with bradycardia, please refer to Newborn Resuscitation protocol, Lavender 5.

Concerning signs or symptoms: hypotension, altered mental status, syncope/presyncope, chest pain, dyspnea, acute heart failure, signs of shock, or cyanosis/pallor:

If NO concerning signs or symptoms, then all levels (EMT/Advanced EMT/Paramedic) may do the following:

  1. O₂ as appropriate.
  2. Advanced EMT/Paramedic ONLY – consider 20 mL/kg IV/IO fluid bolus.

If ANY concerning signs or symptoms, then:

EMT

  1. Airway management per Airway Algorithm, Blue 2.
  2. Request ALS.
  3. Initiate chest compressions for patients with a HR less than 60 beats per minute despite adequate oxygenation and ventilation.
  4. Perform 12-lead ECG, if so trained.

ADVANCED EMT
5. IV/IO en route and fluid bolus as required.
6. Cardiac monitor, perform 12-lead ECG, if so trained.
7. Request ALS.

PARAMEDIC
8. If no response to oxygenation, ventilation and chest compressions:
a. EPINEPHrine [1 mg/10mL] 0.01 mg/kg (0.1 mL/kg) IV/IO, MAX single dose 1 mg. Repeat every 3-5 minutes.
b. In addition, consider Atropine* for increased vagal tone or primary AV Block Atropine 0.02 mg/kg IV/IO. May repeat x 1. Minimum dose 0.1 mg and MAX single dose 0.5 mg.(*refer to PEARLS, Red 26)
9. Apply external pacer. Initiate transcutaneous pacing (TCP) for patients who do not respond to pharmacologic interventions.
a. Consider premedicating with:
i. Midazolam 0.05 mg/kg IV/IO or 0.1 mg/kg IN (MAX dose 3 mg) OR
ii. Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg IV/IN/IO to a MAX first dose of 100 mcg.
b. Contact OLMC as soon as possible
11. Check for capture frequently (every 2 minutes) by checking peripheral pulses, ensuring the pulse matches the paced rate.
12. If pulseless arrest develops, follow Pediatric Cardiac Arrest protocol, Red 18.
