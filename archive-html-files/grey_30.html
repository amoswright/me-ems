EMS Communication Card
For Medical, Physical, and Emotional Information

INSTRUCTIONS: Have the patient touch pictures to request wants and needs. If unable, the caregiver touches pictures and gets yes slash no response from patient. This may be helpful for patients who are deaf, hard of hearing, or could have traumatic brain injury, autism, or an intellectual disability.

Yes = thumbs up, nod, or eyes wide open No = thumbs down, nod, or eyes closed

Quick communication tips

  • Get my attention first.

  • Make eye contact when you speak.

  • Speak clearly at a normal pace and tone.

  • Do not cover your mouth.

  • I may have memory issues and it may take me longer to process what you are telling me.

  • I cannot lipread everything you say.

  • A cochlear implant or hearing aid does not mean I can hear and understand everything you say.

  • I may only understand sign language.
