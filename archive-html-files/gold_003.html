Allergy/Anaphylaxis #3

Anaphylaxis is highly likely when EITHER of the following criteria are met:

  1. Acute onset of illness (minutes to hours) with skin or mucosal involvement* AND either of the following:

    1. Respiratory compromise**
    2. Hypotension or evidence of end-organ dysfunction***

  2. Acute onset of illness (minutes to hours) after exposure to a likely allergen AND any two of the following:

    1. Skin or mucosal changes*
    2. Respiratory compromise**
    3. Hypotension or evidence of end-organ dysfunction***
    4. Persistent GI symptoms****

*Skin or mucosal tissue changes include hives, pruritis, flushing, swelling of the lips/tongue/uvula, etc.

**Respiratory compromise refers to dyspnea, wheezing, bronchospasm, stridor, hypoxia, etc.

***Hypotension or evidence of end-organ dysfunction may include systolic BP of less than 90 mmHg (or greater than 30% decrease from baseline BP) in adults OR BP less than age-specific cut-off in pediatric patients, refer to Normal Pediatric Vital Signs, Pink 4; hypotonia; collapse; syncope, etc. <img>Bear</img>

****Abdominal pain, vomiting, diarrhea
