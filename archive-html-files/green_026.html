Facial/Dental Injury

EMT/ADVANCED EMT/ PARAMEDIC

  1. Administer oxygen, as appropriate, with a target of achieving 94-99% saturation.
  2. Avulsed tooth:
    1. Avoid touching the root of the avulsed tooth. Do not wipe off tooth.
    2. Pick up at crown end. If dirty, rinse off under cold water for 10 seconds.
    3. Place tooth in milk or saline as the storage medium. Alternatively, a cooperative patient can hold tooth in mouth using own saliva as storage medium.
  3. Unstable Mandible Injury:
    1. Expect patient cannot spit/swallow effectively and have suction readily available.
    2. Preferentially transport sitting up with emesis basin/suction available (in the absence of a suspected spinal injury, see Spinal Management Protocol, Green 3).
  4. Epistaxis:
    1. Pinch nose continuously for 10 – 15 minutes, below nasal bones while having patient lean forward. May place a nasal clamp for hemorrhage, if so available.
  5. Nose/Ear Avulsion:
    1. Recover tissue if doing so does not delay care.
    2. Transport with avulsed tissue wrapped in dry sterile gauze in a plastic bag placed on ice.
    3. Severe ear and nose lacerations can be covered with a moist sterile dressing.

ADVANCED EMT/PARAMEDIC

  1. IV access, as needed, for fluid or medication administration.

PARAMEDIC

  1. Pain medication as per Universal Pain Management protocol, Green 18.
