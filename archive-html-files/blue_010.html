Pulmonary Edema (without shock)

Avoid nitroglycerin in any patient who has used a phosphodiesterase inhibitor within the past 48 hours. Examples are: sildenafil (Viagra, Revatio), vardenafil (Levitra, Staxyn), tadalafil (Cialis, Adcirca) which are used for erectile dysfunction and pulmonary hypertension. Contact OLMC for options in patients who have taken such medicines.

If initial systolic BP is less than 100 mmHg, refer to Cardiogenic Shock protocol, Red 32.

EMT

  1. O₂ as appropriate. Assist ventilations (PPV) if needed.
  2. Assess for shock.
  3. If BP greater than 100 mmHg, place in sitting position.
  4. Request ALS.
  5. If available, and so trained, consider trial of CPAP.
  6. Obtain 12-lead ECG, if available and so trained.

ADVANCED EMT
7. Cardiac monitor and 12-lead ECG.
8. Establish IV en route.
9. Manage airway as needed, refer to Airway Algorithm, Blue 2.

  1. Contact OLMC for administration of nitroglycerin 0.4 mg tab or 1 spray sublingual (SL). Repeat nitroglycerin at 2-minute intervals if systolic BP greater than 100 mmHg. After initiation of SL nitroglycerin, may place 1 inch of nitroglycerin ointment 2% to the chest wall if BP greater than 100 mmHg and remove nitroglycerin ointment 2% if BP less than 100 mmHg. If the patient has had nitroglycerin before and no IV is established, and systolic BP is greater than 100 mmHg, then it is OK to give nitroglycerin.

  2. Consider use of CPAP

PARAMEDIC

  1. Paramedic may perform all treatments above without medical control.

  2. Contact OLMC if above measures are not working.
