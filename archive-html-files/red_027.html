If hemodynamically STABLE:
14. Narrow complex tachycardia (due to rhythms other than sinus tachycardia):
a. Modified Valsalva maneuver (if patient able to follow directions and participate) - Position patient seated upright and have patient blow into a 10 mL syringe forcefully, attempting to move the plunger for 15 seconds. Then, immediately lay the patient supine and raise legs to 45 degrees. Hold legs for 1 min, then return to seated position. Reassess. May repeat once.

Then, immediately lay the patient supine and raise legs to 45 degrees. Hold legs for 1 min, then return to seated position.*

b. For patients unable to perform the Modified Valsalva maneuver, consider any of the following:
i. Place a chilled ice pack (if available) gently on the infant’s eyes and cheeks for 15–30 seconds (being careful not to occlude the nose/mouth)
ii. Gently press the infant’s knees to the chest for 15-30 seconds
iii. Ask the infant to blow forcefully through a straw or into a syringe
c. Adenosine*:
i. Adenosine 0.1 mg/kg IV/IO (MAX dose 6 mg) rapid bolus at centrally located peripheral IV with rapid saline flush.
ii. May repeat adenosine x 1 at 0.2 mg/kg IV/IO (MAX dose of 12 mg) rapid bolus at centrally located peripheral IV preferred with rapid saline flush.
d. If no IV/IO access or adenosine ineffective
i. Synchronized cardioversion at 0.5-1.0 J/kg.
ii. If first attempt unsuccessful, increase to 2.0 J/kg for all subsequent attempts.
