Hemorrhagic Shock #1

If history of illness or mechanism of injury consistent with signs/symptoms of shock (elevated pulse, elevated respiratory rate, cool/pale skin, altered LOC, anxiety, sweating or lowered BP) then transport as soon and as efficiently as possible.

If the cause of the shock is NOT related to hemorrhage , consider the following protocols:
Anaphylaxis, refer to the Anaphylaxis protocol, Gold 1
Cardiogenic, refer to the Cardiogenic Shock protocol, Red 32
Tension pneumothorax, refer to the Chest Trauma protocol, Green 7
Medical shock, refer to the Medical Shock protocol, Gold 14

EMT

  1. Control external bleeding, refer to Hemorrhage protocol, Green 12.
  2. Manage airway, as appropriate; see Airway Algorithm, Blue 2.
  3. If patient is in third trimester of pregnancy:
    a. Place patient in left or right lateral recumbent position and re-evaluate.
    b. If shock is secondary to trauma, immobilize patient on a backboard before placing in left or right lateral recumbent position (manually displace uterus to the left or right if tilting the board is not possible).
  4. If the cause of hypovolemic shock is felt to be secondary to acute unstable pelvic fracture, apply a Maine EMS-approved pelvic stabilization device. If Maine EMS-approved pelvic binder is not available, immobilize with a sheet wrap.
  5. Request ALS.

ADVANCED EMT/PARAMEDIC
6. Establish 2 large bore IVs en route.
7. Cardiac monitor
8. If shock present (see table on Hemorrhagic Shock #3), perform fluid bolus according to the following guidelines:
a. Pediatrics – establish IV access and perform 20 mL/kg fluid bolus. Repeat, as needed, within 15-30 min for a total of 60 mL/kg.
b. Uncontrolled bleeding: for suspected internal bleeding or uncontrolled bleeding, fluid bolus to maintain age appropriate target systolic BP (90 mmHg in adults).
c. In suspected TBI/CNS injury initiate fluid bolus to maintain age-appropriate SBP. Refer to Head Trauma protocol, Green 8, for age-appropriate SBP.

Current evidence suggests there may be a benefit from use of Lactated Ringers in critically ill patients with shock. Consider using Lactated Ringers preferentially, if available, while still aiming for age-appropriate BP in hemorrhagic shock and head injuries.
9. Contact OLMC if patient is older than 65 years of age for fluid bolus order
10. Contact OLMC if blood pressure remains less than 90 mmHg systolic after fluid boluses
