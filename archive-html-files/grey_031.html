Care of the Visually Impaired and Blind

EMT/Advanced EMT/Paramedic

  1. Introduce yourself and announce to patient every time you enter or leave the environment of care
  2. Ensure the patient has access to mobility equipment (e.g., cane, guide dog) at all times.
  3. If assisting with ambulation, move slowly and guide the patient while describing potential obstacles (e.g., stairs, slopes, changes in flooring).
  4. Ensure the patient has access to personal technology (e.g., phone, smartwatch)
  5. Tell the patient what you are going to do before doing it.
  6. Listen to the patient's caregiver(s), if the patient consents and if applicable, but only after gathering as much information from the patient as you can.
  7. Notify the hospital of incoming patient with special needs and desire for advocate, if requested by patient. <img>Hospital Logo</img>
  8. Accommodate the needs of the patient, but do not over-focus on visual impairment during obtaining the history of present illness.
  9. Clearly note the patient's visual impairment in the run report.
