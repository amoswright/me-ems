NOTE: For all cases, attempt to identify and treat the underlying cause of the patient's tachycardia which may include maximizing oxygenation or (for Advanced EMTs/Paramedics) maximizing hemodynamics. In the stable patient, a heart rate of less than 180 in children and less than 220 in infants is probable sinus tachycardia if p-waves are present and normal in appearance. If uncertainty exists between sinus tachycardia and SVT, please contact OLMC.

EMT

  1. Airway management per Airway Algorithm, Blue 2.
  2. Request ALS.
  3. Perform 12-lead ECG, if so trained.

ADVANCED EMT
4. Establish IV/IO.
5. Cardiac monitor, perform 12-lead ECG.
6. Request ALS.

PARAMEDIC

If hemodynamically UNSTABLE, as manifested by any of the following: hypotension, altered mental status, syncope/pre- syncope, chest pain, dyspnea, acute heart failure, signs of shock:

For NARROW complex/probable SVT
7. If IV/IO access present:
a. Adenosine* 0.1 mg/kg IV/IO (MAX dose 6 mg) rapid bolus at centrally located peripheral IV preferred with rapid saline flush.
b. May repeat adenosine x 1 at 0.2 mg/kg IV/IO (MAX dose 12 mg) rapid bolus at centrally located peripheral IV with rapid saline flush.
8. If no IV/IO access or adenosine ineffective:
a. Synchronized cardioversion at 0.5 - 1.0 J/kg.
b. If first attempt unsuccessful, increase to 2.0 J/kg for all subsequent attempts.
9. Consider pre-medication, if feasible
a. Midazolam 0.05 mg/kg IV/IO or 0.1 mg/kg IN (MAX dose 3 mg) OR
b. Fentanyl 1 mcg/kg IV/IN/IO to a MAX first dose of 100 mcg

For WIDE complex tachycardia
10. Synchronized cardioversion at 0.5 - 1.0 J/kg.
a. If first attempt unsuccessful, increased to 2.0 J/kg for all subsequent attempts.
11. Contact OLMC for consideration of amiodarone 5 mg/kg IV/IO (MAX dose 300 mg) over 20-60 minutes (mix in D₅W).
12. If unable to synchronize, or in the case of patient instability or polymorphic VT, defibrillate at 2 J/kg with up to a max of 10 J/kg.
13. If pulseless arrest develops, follow Pediatric Cardiac Arrest protocol, Red 18. (Continued)
