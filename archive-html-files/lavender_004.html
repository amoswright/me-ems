Newborn Metrics

Assess the baby at 1 minute and again at 5 minutes.

DO NOT DELAY RESUSCITATION to obtain APGAR Score.

A score of less than 7 suggests need for resuscitation with suction, ventilation, and ALS back up.

Initial ventilations in a newborn should be with ROOM AIR. Often, the cause of respiratory distress/bradycardia is due to the amniotic fluid in the alveoli. Newborns are often able to clear this fluid by with crying and taking in deep breaths. Therefore, it is important to adequately dry and stimulate the infant for the first 30 seconds. If HR is less that 100 bpm after 30 seconds of drying and stimulation, ventilate via BVM on room air. If after 30 seconds of PPV with room air they have a heart rate below 100, ventilate with 100% oxygen. See Lavender 5.
