Aircraft Arrival

  • Identify Scene and LZ Incident Command
  • Establish radio communications prior to landing
  • State Fire or State EMS are the default radio frequencies
  • Advise pilot of terrain conditions, vertical obstructions, and wind direction
  • Secure LZ and appoint personnel to guard main and tail rotors (i.e., Rotor Officer) as per current ground safety course
  • Notify pilot if patient is packaged and ready for hot load

Operating Around Helicopter

  • Approach aircraft with crew escort only
  • Approach aircraft 90 degrees to door only
  • Avoid tail boom and rotor at all times
  • Eye and ear protection should be worn
  • Do not carry anything above shoulder height
  • Secure all loose medical and personnel equipment
  • Spotlights, headlights, and/or hand-held lights should not be pointed directly at the helicopter
